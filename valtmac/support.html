Frequently Asked Questions

How is my data encrypted?

Valt uses AES-256-GCM encryption with Argon2id key derivation. Your master password is never stored — only a derived key is used to encrypt and decrypt your vault.

Where is my data stored?

All vault data is stored locally on your Mac in encrypted form. No data is ever sent to our servers. If you enable iCloud Sync, encrypted data is synced via Apple's iCloud infrastructure.

What if I forget my master password?

If you set up a recovery key during onboarding, you can use it to reset your master password without losing data. If you don't have a recovery key, you can erase the vault and start fresh from the unlock screen.

How do I set up AutoFill?

Go to System Settings > Passwords > AutoFill Passwords and Passkeys, then enable Valt as a credential provider. After that, Valt can fill passwords in Safari and other browsers.

Can I import passwords from other managers?

Yes. Valt supports importing from 1Password, LastPass, Bitwarden, Chrome, and other password managers via CSV files.

How do I export my data?

You can export your vault as an encrypted backup or as a CSV file from Settings > Import & Export.

Is iCloud Sync secure?

Yes. Only encrypted data is synced via iCloud. Your encryption keys never leave your device. Even Apple cannot read your vault data.
